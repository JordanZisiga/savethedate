Getting There

Fly into Nairobi's Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO). We recommend arriving at least on Thursday with a buffer night for a well rested start.

The easiest way to get around is Uber. It's easily available, reliable, and very affordable. You'll see Uber Chapchap which are smaller budget Ubers perfect for short trips. And if you're feeling adventurous, Matatus are iconic and could in theory get you where you want. But just use Uber.

Before You Go
Do I need a visa?
Most likely, yes. Kenya's e-visa costs $30 and takes about 10 minutes to apply. Do it at least a week before you fly.
When should I arrive?
We recommend arriving Thursday, November 26 and staying in Nairobi that night. You'll be rested and ready for the shuttle to Naivasha on Friday.
What should I pack?

Join our WhatsApp group before you travel. If you miss a shuttle, contact us ‚Äî we'll help arrange alternatives.
Mosquitos & malaria?
Not too bad this time of year. If malaria medication is recommended for your situation, talk to your doctor before the trip.
Money, power & phone
Credit cards work in Nairobi. Some Kenyan Shillings in cash is handy. Kenya uses Type G (UK-style) plugs. Most international phone plans work, or you can grab a local SIM easily.
